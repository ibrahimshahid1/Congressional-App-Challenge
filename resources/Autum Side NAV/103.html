Its interesting that early accounts of this cluster record that it was a nebula, however its an Open Cluster. Although muh smaller than the full moon, m103 is an easy target for binoculars. To find m103, pan your telescope between Segin and Ruchbah but stay closer to Ruchbah.

Messiers Comments: Méchain) `Cluster of stars between Epsilon and Delta of the leg of Cassiopeia.'

Fun Facts: This loose open cluster was discovered by P. Méchain in 1781. It is one of the more distance open clusters in the Messier Catalog. According to Kharchenko et al. (2005), the distance of M103 is 7150 light years and its diameter is 17 light years. It contains 77 stars and its estimated age is 16-25 million years.
