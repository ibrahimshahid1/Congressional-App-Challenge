Messier 45

M45: The Pleiades, M45, is probably the best-known Messier Object, This tight cluster of bright stars is best known since antiquity, and is found in the mythologies of many cultures. May often refer to the open cluster as the 7 sisters or the Subaru. The open cluster is also very prominent in the winter sky. The cluster is in the constellation of Taurus and is visible even under light polluted skies. Since the Pleiades lies near the ecliptic, it sometimes found near the Moon or planets. On dark transparent nights, many have been able to see nebulosity around the main stars in a 4in+ telescope. Beginner stargazers often confuse M45 with the little dipper. To the naked eye, only 6 to 7 stars may be visible, binoculars ma y reveals additional background stars. To view the cluster in its entirety, use your lowest power eyepiece.

Fun Facts: The faint reflection nebula visible around the brightest stars is an unrelated dust cloud that the cluster is currently passing through. Gravitational interactions with other stars in the galactic neighborhood will eventually disperse the cluster about 250 million years in the future

Messiers Comments: March 4, 1769
`A cluster of stars, known by the name of the Pleiades. The position reported is that of the star Alcyone.'
