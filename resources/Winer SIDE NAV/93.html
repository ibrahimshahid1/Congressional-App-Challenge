Messier 93

As you can see in the picture, M74 is between Aries and Pisces, but is closer to the star Alpherg in Pisces. Point your telescope at Alpherg, and then try rotating your telescope left if using a dobsonian.

Cameras should easily be able to to pick up the galaxys spiral structure. In the eyepiece, you should be able to see a faint halo around the core and nothing else. This is due to the fact that we humans cannot take long exposures of images, but any camera (ex.Iphone) can take up to 3 seconds of exposure but more professional DSLR cameras can take 1 hour + exposure shots.

M74 is best viewed in late evening during atutumn. If your participating in a mid-March messier matarthon, M74 and M77 should be one of the first targest as they are some of the brightest Messier objects and are therefore the easiest to see.

All seasonal objects like M74 will always appear at their highest point in the southern sky.

Messiers Description: Nebula without stars, near the star Eta Piscium, seen by M. Méchain at the end of September 1780, and he reports: "This nebula doesn't contain any stars; it is fairly large, very obscure, and extremely difficult to observe; one can recognize it with more certainty in fine, frosty conditions". M. Messier looked for it and found it, as M. Méchain describes it: it has been compared directly
with the star Eta Piscium.'

M74 is also known as the phantom galaxy as it can only be seen under very dark sky sites by the naked eye. Several astronomers, beginner and professional, often mistaken M74 as a globular cluster.

More about M74:The remarkably symmetric appearance over the whole galaxy is probably caused by the global phenomenon of density waves sweeping around M74's gaseous disk, probably induced by gravitaional interaction with neighboring galaxies. When gas clouds orbiting within the disk encounter such density waves, they are accelerated into the spiral shaped wave crest, and then slowed down, so that they converge toward the spiral arm, enhance the density wave. Moreover, collisions and mergers of neighboring clouds occur, which are thought to induce the observed starbirth activity along the spiral arms.
