Messier 38

This open clsuters main stars form a prominent X shape, but that patternn may become obstructed under very dark skys, where more background stars might beomce visible. While your in the area, see if you can locate the open cluseter NGC1907. It may be in the same field of view as M38 at low magnification. 

M38 is found near the cneter of the hexagon of  stars that make up the constelltion or Aurgiam midway betweeen Mahasim and Hasseleah. Try finding the mid point between Mahasim and Hasseleh and span you telescope right to left.

Fun Facts: In spite of its inclusion in the Messier Catalog , this open cluster  was actually discovered by G. B. Hodierna in 1654. Along with M36  and M37 , it forms a trio of open clusters in Auriga. According to Kharchenko et al. (2005) , the distance of M38 is 3480 light years and its diameter is 15 light years. It contains over 100 stars and its estimated age is 150-250 million years.

Messiers Comments: September 25, 1764
`Cluster of small stars in Auriga, near the star Sigma, little distant from the two preceding clusters [M36 and M37]; this one is of square shape and contains no nebulosity, if one takes care to examine it with a good telescope. Its extension is about 15' of arc.' (diam. 15')
